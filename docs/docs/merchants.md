# Merchants

A merchant in Surfboard refers to a business entity onboarded directly by us or through our partners (ISVs, payment facilitators, or institutional partners) to utilize our payment products and services. Merchants can use our payment products and services to accept payments for goods and services, receive settlements for the payments collected, access reporting tools for settlement reconciliation and management, and much more.

## Merchant attributes

The following information will be associated with each merchant in Surfboard:

-   **MCC**: MCC or Merchant Category Code, is a four-digit identifier used to categorize merchants based on their primary product or service offering.
-   **Transaction Currency**: Transaction currency is the currency used to denote the value of goods or services being sold in a transaction.
-   **Settlement Currency:** Settlement currency is the currency used to facilitate the settlement of financial transactions between the merchants and the acquiring bank. It represents the currency in which merchants receive their funds during payout.
-   **Settlement Bank Accounts**: A settlement bank account is the merchant's designated bank account where the funds are transferred post-settlement. Once a customer's payment has been settled through the acquiring bank, the settled funds are deposited into this account.

## Merchant capabilities

Merchants in Surfboard will be able to:

-   **Create and manage stores**: Merchants can create [**stores**](/docs/stores) for each physical or virtual location of sale. Merchants can also manage their stores from the Merchant Dashboard.
-   **Receive terminals**: Surfboard provides terminals for merchants to process payments. The terminals are shipped to merchants once they have been onboarded. After registering the terminals to a store, merchants can use them to accept payments.
-   **Create users for the stores**: Merchants have the flexibility to create and manage user accounts for individual stores. This helps in assigning roles and responsibilities to different team members for better store management.
-   **Accept payments**: By using Surfboard’s terminals, merchants can accept [**payments**](/docs/payments) for their products and services. Merchants can also enable various payment methods(Card, Cash, Swish, and AMEX) from the Merchant Dashboard.
-   **Receive payouts**: Surfboard ensures secure and timely [**settlements**](/docs/settlements) and payouts to the merchants. Funds collected through the stores are securely paid out to the merchant's linked bank account.
-   **Receive invoices, and reports for settlement reconciliation**: Merchants are provided with detailed [**reports**](/docs/reporting), to reconcile settlements and maintain clear and accurate financial records.
-   **Fetch and display all stores**: Merchants can easily retrieve and view all their stores in the Merchant Dashboard. This simplifies the management of multiple stores, providing an overview at a glance.
